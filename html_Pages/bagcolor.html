WHAT IS COLOR THEORY?

Color theory is a fundamental aspect of design, art, and aesthetics that explores how colors interact, evoke emotions, and communicate messages. Understanding color theory allows us to make intentional choices when it comes to color.
In this guide, we will dive into the world of color, exploring how to achieve the girly girl vibe.

COLOR THEORY TYPS:

Split-Complementary Color

A split-complementary color scheme is known to be a variation of a complementary color scheme.But rather than being a mixture of two colors, split-complementary colors contain a combination of three colors.

Triadic Color

A triadic color scheme consists of three colors. Unlike colors that sit next to or directly across each other, triadic colors are equidistant on the color wheel. When you join these colors, they form a triangle.

Analogous

These color schemes are most often seen in nature.For example, during the fall, one might often see the changing leaves form an analogous sort of color scheme, progressively moving through the color wheel to create a gradient in its natural pattern.

Double Complementary

In a double complementary scheme, we use a combination of four colors that, as the name implies, is made up of two complementary color pairs.
(Remember, two colors are complementary if they are opposite each other on the color wheel.)
To make it even easier, this kind of color combination is also known as rectangular colors because when the four colors are connected on the color wheel they form a rectangle.

What is personal coloring for?

Have you ever heard about "Personal Colour"? It's color analysis, also known as personal color analysis (PCA), seasonal color analysis, or skin-tone matching, is a term often used within the cosmetics and fashion industry to describe a method of determining the colors of clothing and makeup that harmonize with a person's skin complexion, eye color, and hair color with the benefit of being able to use this tool for wardrobe planning and style consulting.

How to identify your skin's undertone?

Warm Undertone Cool Undertone Clear undertone

If your veins appear more greenish and gold jewelry complements your skin tone, you likely have a warm undertone
If your veins appear bluish and silver jewelry complements your skin tone, you likely have a cool undertone.
If you have difficulty determining whether your veins appear greenish or bluish and both gold and silver jewelry complement your skin tone, you likely have a clear undertone.

Once you have identified your undertone, you can choose colors that enhance your natural beauty.
Here are some general guidelines:

Blue

Blue shades, such as sky blue, royal blue, or navy, can complement cool undertones.

Red

Red shades, such as cherry red, burgundy, or maroon, can complement warm undertones.

Green

Green shades, such as mint green, olive green, or emerald, can complement neutral undertones.

Yellow

Yellow shades, such as lemon yellow, mustard, or goldenrod, can complement warm undertones.

Purple

Purple shades, such as lavender, mauve, or plum, can complement cool undertones.

Orange

Orange shades, such as peach, terracotta, or burnt orange, can complement neutral undertones.

Pink

Pink shades, such as baby pink, rose, or fuchsia, can complement cool undertones.

Black

Black is a versatile and classic color that can complement cool undertones and add sophistication to any look.

White

White is a crisp and clean color that can complement cool undertones and create a fresh and elegant appearance.
